# 30-60-90 Day Plan – No Dedicated Team

> **Purpose:** Outline how meaningful progress is delivered in the first 90 days before a full team is staffed.

---

## First 30 Days: Establish Control and Direction

### Objectives
- Clarify scope and priorities
- Create execution structure
- Deliver first visible outputs

### Key Actions
- Confirm department charter and success metrics
- Implement intake and prioritization process
- Identify 1–2 high-impact workflows
- Conduct focused SME observations (time-boxed)
- Produce initial standard work and job aids

### Outputs
- Intake backlog with prioritization criteria
- First standard work document
- Quick reference guide for priority workflow

---

## Days 31–60: Deliver and Prove Value

### Objectives
- Demonstrate repeatable delivery
- Reduce operational friction
- Establish credibility

### Key Actions
- Release additional job aids or microlearning
- Track adoption and early performance indicators
- Refine templates and standards
- Begin knowledge centralization

### Outputs
- Updated training or process assets
- Early adoption metrics
- Centralized content repository

---

## Days 61–90: Stabilize and Prepare to Scale

### Objectives
- Transition from ad hoc execution to repeatable model
- Support staffing justification
- Reduce SME dependency

### Key Actions
- Formalize review and update cadence
- Identify gaps requiring additional staffing
- Document operating rhythms and roles
- Align next-phase roadmap to outcomes

### Outputs
- Refined operating model
- Staffing justification inputs
- Scalable execution plan

---

## My thought process on this as a Manager

I focus on control, visibility, and early wins. Even without a team, structure creates momentum. The goal is to prove value quickly, reduce noise for operations, and create a system that new hires can step into rather than rebuild.
