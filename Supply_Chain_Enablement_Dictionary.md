# Supply Chain Enablement Dictionary  
(Healthcare Environment)

## Purpose
This dictionary defines key terms, abbreviations, and concepts used throughout the Supply Chain Training and Process Improvement repository. It ensures consistent understanding across Supply Chain, Information Resources (IR), Finance, and institutional stakeholders.

---

## A

**A3**  
A structured problem-solving method used to define problems, analyze root causes, implement countermeasures, and sustain improvements.

**Accounts Payable (AP)**  
The function responsible for processing vendor invoices and ensuring accurate, timely payment.

**Adoption**  
The extent to which users consistently and correctly use a system or process as designed.

---

## B

**BI (Business Intelligence)**  
Tools and practices used to analyze data and present insights to support decision-making.

---

## C

**Change Impact Assessment**  
A structured review identifying who is affected by a system or process change and what readiness actions are required.

**Compliance**  
Adherence to laws, regulations, policies, and internal controls governing healthcare operations.

**Continuous Improvement**  
Ongoing efforts to improve processes, efficiency, quality, and outcomes.

---

## D

**Downstream Impact**  
The effect of upstream actions or errors on subsequent processes or teams.

---

## E

**Enablement**  
The combination of training, tools, SOPs, and support that allows staff to perform effectively.

**eProcurement**  
Electronic systems used to manage requisitions, purchase orders, receiving, and invoicing.

---

## F

**Forum (Enablement Forum)**  
A recurring cross-functional meeting used to align priorities, review metrics, and coordinate training and system readiness.

---

## G

**Go-Live**  
The point at which a system enhancement or process change becomes active in production.

**Governance**  
The structure that defines decision-making authority, accountability, and oversight.

---

## H

**Hypercare**  
A period of heightened support following a go-live to stabilize operations and address issues quickly.

---

## I

**IR (Information Resources)**  
The department responsible for system development, integrations, infrastructure, and technical support.

**Intake (Training Intake)**  
The formal process for submitting and prioritizing training or enablement requests.

---

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**  
A measurable value used to evaluate operational performance and effectiveness.

---

## L

**LMS (Learning Management System)**  
A platform used to assign, track, and record training completion.

---

## M

**Metrics**  
Quantitative measures used to evaluate performance, adoption, and improvement.

---

## O

**Onboarding**  
The structured process of integrating new hires into their roles, systems, and workflows.

---

## P

**Policy**  
A governing rule or principle that directs organizational behavior.

**Process Improvement**  
Structured efforts to reduce variation, waste, errors, or inefficiencies.

**Procurement**  
The function responsible for sourcing goods and services in compliance with contracts and policies.

---

## R

**RACI**  
A responsibility assignment model defining who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.

**Receiving**  
The process of accepting, verifying, and documenting inbound goods.

---

## S

**Service Level Agreement (SLA)**  
Defined expectations for response times, resolution, and service quality.

**SME (Subject Matter Expert)**  
An individual with deep knowledge of a specific process or system.

**SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)**  
Documented instructions that standardize how tasks are performed.

**System Readiness**  
The state in which users, processes, and support structures are prepared for a system change.
